Weather and Flood Update

Severe Rainfall Warning: The IMD has issued an “extremely heavy rainfall” warning for Hyderabad from August 14 to 16. Expect 64mm to over 204mm of rain, thunderstorms, and gusty winds.

Ongoing Flood Risk: Multiple areas have already experienced waterlogging and flash floods. Low-lying neighborhoods are particularly vulnerable.

Forecasted Conditions:

  • August 15 (Friday): Heavy showers, overcast, high humidity. High ~28°C, low ~22°C.
  • August 16 (Saturday): Passing showers, continued disruptions. High ~29°C.
  • August 17 (Sunday): Persistent rain, gradual decrease in intensity. High ~29°C.

Advisories: Expect possible partial road closures, traffic diversions, and detours. Transport, especially in flood-prone areas and older city sections like Charminar, may be affected.

Travel Advice

Key Recommendations

  • Carry rain protection (umbrellas, raincoats, waterproof shoes).
  • Monitor real-time weather and traffic before venturing out.
  • Expect delays at airports, railway stations, and on city roads.
  • Be flexible with plans and consider backup options for outdoor activities.

Safety First

  • Avoid flood-prone and waterlogged areas.
  • Stay updated on official announcements from civic authorities.
  • Exercise caution while traveling, especially in older parts of the city.
Dark Tourism Preparation

Essential Equipment

Rain Protection

  • Sturdy umbrella and/or raincoat/poncho
  • Waterproof backpack or dry bag
  • Waterproof cases for phone and documents

Clothing

  • Quick-dry, lightweight clothing
  • Waterproof shoes or boots with good grip
  • Spare socks and change of clothes

Navigation & Documentation

  • Paper maps and/or offline digital maps
  • Power bank for smartphone
  • Notebook and pen
  • Camera (ideally waterproof)

Health & Safety

  • First aid kit
  • Face masks and hand sanitizer
  • Bottled water and energy snacks
  • Emergency whistle and flashlight

Important Things To Do

  • Research and Planning: Study the history of Hyderabad floods and dark tourism sites. Note which areas are accessible and safe.
  • Safety Precautions: Always check real-time weather and flood advisories. Share your itinerary with a contact. Never wade into water of unknown depth.
  • Local Coordination: Contact local guides knowledgeable in Hyderabad’s history for context and safe access.
  • Responsible Tourism Etiquette: Be respectful at sites of tragedy. Do not interfere with relief operations. Take photographs discreetly.
  • Environmental Awareness: Take all waste with you. Avoid unnecessary disturbance of waterlogged or fragile sites.

Special Notes for Hyderabad (August 2025)

  • Expect heavy-to-extremely heavy rain and periodic flooding.
  • Flooded areas can become health hazards due to water-borne diseases—use sanitizer and avoid eating street food in affected zones.
  • Carry full rain gear and be prepared for impromptu shifts in plans due to weather or civic advisories.

Optional Equipment for Dark Tourism Enthusiasts

  • Voice recorder (for site impressions/interviews)
  • Guidebook on Hyderabad’s disasters/historical tragedies
  • Portable GPS tracker
  • Lightweight folding stool
