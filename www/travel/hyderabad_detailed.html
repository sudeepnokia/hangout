Hyderabad Trip Itinerary
15‑17 Aug 2025 – 7 travellers (two couples & family of three)

This itinerary suggests a relaxed but comprehensive trip to Hyderabad for your group of seven. Arrival and departure occur at Secunderabad Junction (arrival at 07:00 on 15 Aug 2025 and departure at 17:25 on 17 Aug 2025). Distances in Hyderabad are manageable but traffic can be unpredictable, so expect slight delays. Hyderabad Metro is a convenient and eco‑friendly way to explore the city – it runs from around 06:30 to 22:30, with 56 active stations and three lines【745268106529381†screenshot】【211947579895794†L24-L35】; trains run every 4–12 min depending on the time of day【745268106529381†screenshot】. Cabs or a rented 7‑seater (e.g., Toyota Innova/tempo traveller) are recommended when your whole group travels together (especially for longer trips such as Ramoji Film City), while autos or the metro work well for shorter hops.

Accommodation recommendations near Secunderabad

  • Royalton Hotel, Abids: centrally located near the historic Abids area (~6 km from Secunderabad station). Sulekha lists the Royalton Hotel as open 24 hours and gives it a 5/5 rating (based on user reviews)【136264523704591†L71-L79】. Rooms are spacious and the property is near attractions like Charminar, Lumbini Park and the Birla science complex【136264523704591†L126-L133】. Check‑in is typically noon; request early check‑in or leave bags with reception while sightseeing. Approximate tariff for a double room in Aug 2025 is ₹3,500–4,500/night; a family suite for three costs around ₹4,500–5,000.
  • Hotel Sai Krupa, Chikkadpally: a budget‑friendly hotel near Chikkadpally (about 4 km from Secunderabad). It offers A/C and non‑A/C rooms, 24‑hr security, travel desk and an in‑house restaurant【52396398550212†L37-L74】. The hotel is close to Hussain Sagar and Secunderabad station【52396398550212†L39-L47】. Tariff for A/C deluxe rooms is about ₹2,500–3,500/night.
  • Hotel Royal Reve, Clock Tower Secunderabad: 3‑star hotel located on Sarojini Devi Road, a few hundred metres from Secunderabad station. The property offers multiple room categories (standard, executive and suite) with modern amenities, including 24‑hr running water, internet and minibar【458144208107950†L39-L62】. Its central location makes travel to attractions like Golconda Fort (15 km) and Birla Mandir (7 km) convenient【458144208107950†L39-L46】. Expect rates of ₹3,500–4,500/night.

These hotels have received good reviews and are within your budget (< ₹5,000/night). Book three rooms (two double and one triple) to comfortably accommodate two couples and the family of three. Most hotels allow luggage storage after checkout; confirm this during booking.

Day 1 – Friday, 15 Aug 2025

TimeActivity & DetailsTransport notes
07:00 Arrive at Secunderabad Junction. Walk or take a short auto to your hotel. If rooms aren’t ready this early, leave baggage at the reception and freshen up. Hotel is near Secunderabad station (walk/auto 5‑10 min).
08:00 Breakfast near the hotel. For authentic Hyderabad tiffins, try Kamat Hotel (vegetarian canteen at Lakdi Ka Pul) or small eateries along Sarojini Devi Road. Walk/auto.
09:30 – 12:30 Birla Science Museum & Planetarium (near Birla Mandir). The museum presents interactive science exhibits and sections on archaeology, dolls and a space museum【395897018549929†L26-L54】. Entry fee: ₹200 for museum; ₹300 for museum + planetarium【395897018549929†L26-L54】. Opens at 10:30 AM and closes 8 PM【395897018549929†L26-L54】. Take the Hyderabad Metro from Secunderabad East to Lakdi Ka Pul (Blue Line). Metro trains run every 4–12 minutes and operate between 06:30 AM and 10:30 PM【745268106529381†screenshot】. From Lakdi Ka Pul station, it’s a short 600 m auto/ cab ride up the hill to the museum.
12:30 – 13:30 Lunch at Kamat Hotel (vegetarian) or other eateries near Birla Mandir/Birla Science Centre. Kamat is popular for south‑Indian meals. Walk/auto.
14:00 – 15:30 Tank Bund & Hussain Sagar lake. The promenade connects Hyderabad and Secunderabad and features 33 bronze statues of historical personalities and views of the Buddha statue in the lake【11721554785913†L41-L70】. There’s no entry fee and it’s open 8 AM–10 PM【11721554785913†L41-L70】. You can take a boat ride to the giant Buddha statue on the lake (boats depart near Lumbini Park; optional). From Birla centre, hire autos or a cab to Tank Bund (~2 km). For a more leisurely approach, walk down the hill via the main road.
16:00 – 17:30 Durgam Cheruvu Cable Bridge in the HITEC city area. This modern cable‑stayed bridge spans a picturesque lake and has walking and cycling paths; entry is free and it is open 6 AM–11 PM【509724273225406†L29-L58】. The bridge is particularly beautiful in the evening when multi‑coloured lights illuminate it【509724273225406†L65-L110】. Take the metro from Lakdi Ka Pul to Hitec City station (Blue Line) and hire an auto to the bridge (2 km). Because your group is large, consider booking a cab directly from Tank Bund to the bridge (~35 min by car) to stay together.
18:00 – 20:00 Return to hotel; rest. In the evening, stroll around RTC X Road or Ashok Nagar area to sample Hyderabad street food. Try local favourites like pani puris, kebabs and jalebis. Return via cab/metro. Autos are plentiful near Tank Bund and Durgam Cheruvu.

Day 2 – Saturday, 16 Aug 2025

TimeActivity & DetailsTransport notes
07:30 Breakfast at the hotel or nearby.
08:00 – 18:00 Ramoji Film City (full day tour). This massive film-themed park includes movie sets, gardens, stunt shows and rides. The Holiday Carnival Studio tour runs from 9 AM to 9 PM and includes a studio tour, live shows and carnival parade; adult tickets are ₹1,450 and child tickets ₹1,250【85805597646559†L68-L83】. The park is open 9 AM–5:30 PM【85805597646559†L31-L40】. Spend the day exploring Bahubali sets, Movie Magic attractions and the late-afternoon Carnival Parade【85805597646559†L68-L83】. Ramoji Film City is ~30 km from central Hyderabad. Hire a 7‑seater cab or tempo traveller for the day (~1 hr each way). Leave the hotel by 8 AM to reach before the 9 AM opening ceremony. Pack water and sun hats.
18:30 Return to Hyderabad city. Dinner at Hotel Shadab in the old city – widely regarded for its biryani and kebabs; TasteAtlas notes its address at 21‑1‑140‑144, Charminar, High Court Road, Ghansi Bazaar, and says the restaurant is recommended by food critics【582426990950164†L112-L115】. The restaurant is near Charminar; your cab driver can take you there en route back from Ramoji. Expect waiting time during dinner hours.
20:30 Return to hotel and rest.

Day 3 – Sunday, 17 Aug 2025

TimeActivity & DetailsTransport notes
07:00 Check out of the hotel. Leave luggage at the reception (most hotels offer luggage storage). Have breakfast nearby.
08:30 – 10:00 Charminar – Hyderabad’s iconic monument built in 1591 by Mohammed Quli Qutb Shah【460888795650487†L62-L79】. The square structure with four 56‑m high minarets has a mosque and viewing gallery. Entry fee: ₹25 for Indian visitors (cashless ₹20) and ₹300 for foreigners【460888795650487†L25-L55】; open 9 AM–5:30 PM every day【460888795650487†L25-L55】. Take a cab/auto to the old city (~7 km from Secunderabad). The area is busy, so it’s easier to use a taxi/tempo rather than metro with a large group. Wear modest clothing and comfortable shoes.
10:00 – 11:00 Laad Bazaar (optional) – a historic market adjacent to Charminar that specialises in lacquer bangles, zari embroidery and pearls【354272426164731†L50-L79】. The bazaar dates to the Qutub Shahi and Nizam eras and spans four roads【354272426164731†L50-L66】. It is open 11 AM–11 PM【354272426164731†L25-L44】. If you love shopping, spend some time here; otherwise proceed to the next attraction. Walk from Charminar (located right next to the monument). Bargain politely.
11:00 – 13:00 Chowmahalla Palace. This former seat of the Asaf Jahi dynasty contains grand halls, a clock tower, courtyards and vintage cars. It is open Saturday–Thursday, 10 AM–5 PM (closed Friday)【638396711076897†L69-L84】. Admission: ₹100 per adult, ₹40 for children under 10 and ₹400 for foreigners; photography permit ₹50【638396711076897†L69-L84】. Plan to spend ~2 hours exploring the palace interiors and gardens【638396711076897†L69-L84】. Chowmahalla Palace is about 700 m south of Charminar. Walk or hire autos. There is a parking lot if your cab accompanies you【638396711076897†L130-L152】.
13:00 – 14:00 Lunch at Hotel Shadab (if not visited earlier) or at local eateries in the old city (try haleem or biryani).
14:00 – 15:30 Salar Jung Museum – one of the world’s largest private art collections. Exhibits include European sculptures, Indian miniature paintings, carpets, clocks and the famous double‑sided marble statue of Veiled Rebecca【791640438636976†L25-L40】. The museum is open 10 AM–5 PM every day except Friday; entry fee is ₹50 for Indian adults, ₹20 for children and ₹500 for foreigners【791640438636976†L48-L84】. The museum is roughly 1.5 km from Chowmahalla Palace; take autos or your cab. Keep at least 1.5 hours to appreciate the highlights.
15:30 – 16:00 Return to the hotel, collect luggage and settle any remaining bills.
16:00 – 17:25 Depart for Secunderabad Junction. Arrive by 17:00 to board your return train at 17:25. Factor in traffic; it can take 30–45 minutes from the old city. Hire a cab or auto. Remember to keep your reserved train tickets handy.

Food Recommendations

  • Hotel Shadab (Old City) – recommended by food critics for its Hyderabadi biryani, haleem and kebabs【582426990950164†L112-L115】. Address: 21‑1‑140‑144, Charminar, High Court Road, Ghansi Bazaar, Hyderabad. It is near Charminar.
  • Street food at RTC X Road/Ashok Nagar – vibrant evening scene with pani puri, dosas, kebabs and sweets.
  • Kamat Hotel (Lakdi Ka Pul) – popular vegetarian eatery near Birla Mandir and the Science Museum. Ideal for breakfast or lunch.

Additional Notes & Tips

  • Weather: Mid‑August falls in the monsoon season; expect occasional showers. Carry umbrellas, raincoats and comfortable footwear.
  • Dress code: Many historical sites and religious places expect modest attire (cover shoulders and knees).
  • Transport: Hyderabad Metro is reliable and air‑conditioned; however, with a group of seven, consider hiring a 7‑seater cab or tempo traveller for the whole day for convenience. Autos are widely available for short journeys. The metro has three lines and 56 active stations; trains run from about 06:30 AM to 10:30 PM【745268106529381†screenshot】【211947579895794†L24-L35】.
  • Entry fees: Carry cash in small denominations for entry tickets and autos. Some monuments offer lower fees for cashless payments (e.g., Charminar charges ₹20 via cashless vs ₹25 cash【460888795650487†L25-L55】).
  • Safety: Hyderabad is generally safe, but keep an eye on personal belongings, especially in crowded markets like Laad Bazaar.
