Build Your Skills: Vertical Circular Motion

This guide will build your understanding from the basics of energy conservation to solving a JEE-level problem about a pendulum.

Phase 1: Conservation of Mechanical Energy

When only gravity is doing work, the total mechanical energy (Kinetic + Potential) of an object remains constant. E = KE + PE = constant.

We define Kinetic Energy as KE = (1/2)mv² and Potential Energy as PE = mgh. We can set the lowest point of the motion (point A) as our reference height, so h=0 and PE_A = 0.

Question 1.1: In the diagram, what is the height (h) of point C above point A? What is the height of point B?
Show Solution

Point C is at the same level as the center O, so its height is the length of the string, h_C = l.

Point B is at an angle of 60° from the bottom. Its height is h_B = l - l*cos(60°) = l - l/2 = l/2.

Phase 2: When Does the String Go Slack?

In vertical circular motion, the tension in the string provides some of the necessary centripetal force. The string goes slack at the exact moment the tension (T) becomes zero.

The equation for tension at an angle θ from the top is T = mv²/l - mgcosθ. When T=0, the speed at that point is given by v_slack² = gl*cos(θ_from_top).

Question 2.1: The problem states the string goes slack at point D, which is at an angle of 60° from the top vertical. What is the square of the speed at D (v_D²)?
Show Solution

Here, θ_from_top = 60°.

v_D² = gl * cos(60°) = gl * (1/2) = gl/2.

The kinetic energy at D is KE_D = (1/2)mv_D² = mgl/4.

Phase 3: Calculating the Total Energy

We can find the total energy of the system by calculating the energy at point D. This total energy will be the same at every other point (A, B, C).

Question 3.1: What is the height of point D above point A? What is the total energy of the system?
Show Solution

The height of D is h_D = l + l*cos(60°) = l + l/2 = 3l/2.

The potential energy at D is PE_D = mgh_D = 3mgl/2.

The kinetic energy at D (from Phase 2) is KE_D = mgl/4.

Total Energy E = PE_D + KE_D = 3mgl/2 + mgl/4 = 7mgl/4.

Phase 4: The JEE Main Question

Now we can use the total energy to find the kinetic energy at points B and C.

Question 31: ... The ratio of the kinetic energy of the bob at the points B and C is:
Show Solution

Step 1: Find the kinetic energy at B.

KE_B = E_total - PE_B. From Phase 1, h_B = l/2, so PE_B = mgl/2.

KE_B = 7mgl/4 - mgl/2 = 7mgl/4 - 2mgl/4 = 5mgl/4.

Step 2: Find the kinetic energy at C.

KE_C = E_total - PE_C. From Phase 1, h_C = l, so PE_C = mgl.

KE_C = 7mgl/4 - mgl = 7mgl/4 - 4mgl/4 = 3mgl/4.

Step 3: Calculate the ratio.

Ratio = KE_B / KE_C = (5mgl/4) / (3mgl/4) = 5/3.
