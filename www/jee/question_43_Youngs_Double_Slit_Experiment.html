Learning Guide: Interference and Young's Double Slit Experiment

This guide explains the famous experiment that demonstrated the wave nature of light and how the pattern changes in different media.

Phase 1: The Basic Setup and Fringe Width

In Young's Double Slit Experiment (YDSE), light passes through two narrow, parallel slits, creating a pattern of bright and dark bands (fringes) on a screen. This pattern is caused by the interference of light waves from the two slits.

The fringe width (β) is the distance between the centers of two consecutive bright fringes (or two consecutive dark fringes).

Fringe Width Formula: β = λD / d

  • β: The fringe width.
  • λ: The wavelength of the light.
  • D: The distance from the slits to the screen.
  • d: The distance between the two slits.
Warm-up Question: In a YDSE setup, if you use light with a longer wavelength, what happens to the distance between the bright fringes?

From the formula β = λD / d, we can see that the fringe width β is directly proportional to the wavelength λ.

So, if you use light with a longer wavelength, the fringe width will increase. The bright fringes will be spaced further apart.

Phase 2: A Step Up - Light Traveling Through a Medium

When light enters a medium (like water or glass) from a vacuum (or air), two things change:

  1. Its speed decreases.
  2. Its wavelength decreases.

The one thing that does not change is its frequency (f). The frequency is determined by the source of the light and remains constant.

The refractive index (n) of a medium tells us how much the speed and wavelength change.

n = (Speed of light in vacuum) / (Speed of light in medium) = c / v

n = (Wavelength in vacuum) / (Wavelength in medium) = λ_vac / λ_med

This gives us a crucial relationship: λ_med = λ_vac / n. The wavelength inside a medium is shorter than in a vacuum.

Reason (R): The speed of light reduces in an optically denser medium than air while its frequency does not change.

Question: Is the statement given in the Reason (R) true or false?

This is the fundamental definition of what happens when light enters a denser medium. The speed slows down, the wavelength gets shorter, but the frequency (the number of wave crests passing a point per second) stays the same. The statement is a correct piece of physics theory.

Therefore, Reason (R) is true.

Phase 3: Putting It All Together

What happens if you perform the entire YDSE experiment submerged in a medium with refractive index 'n' (like water)?

The only thing in our fringe width formula that changes is the wavelength of the light. The distances D and d stay the same.

The new wavelength is λ' = λ_air / n.

So the new fringe width, β', will be:

β' = λ'D / d = (λ_air / n) * D / d = (λ_air * D / d) / n

β' = β_air / n

Since for a denser medium n > 1, the new fringe width β' will be smaller than the fringe width in air. The fringes get closer together.

Assertion (A): If Young's double slit experiment is performed in an optically denser medium than air, then the consecutive fringes come closer.

Question: Is the Assertion (A) true or false?

As we derived in the concept, performing the experiment in a denser medium (n > 1) reduces the fringe width by a factor of n (β' = β_air / n).

If the fringe width is smaller, it means the fringes are closer together.

Therefore, Assertion (A) is true.

Phase 4: The Summit - The JEE Main Question

Original Question: Given below are two statements : one is labelled as Assertion (A) and the other is labelled as Reason (R).

Assertion (A) : If Young's double slit experiment is performed in an optically denser medium than air, then the consecutive fringes come closer.

Reason (R) : The speed of light reduces in an optically denser medium than air while its frequency does not change.

In the light of the above statements, choose the most appropriate answer from the options given below.

Let's analyze both statements and their relationship.

1. Check if A is true: From Phase 3, we confirmed that in a denser medium, the fringe width decreases (β' = β/n), so the fringes come closer. Assertion (A) is true.

2. Check if R is true: From Phase 2, we confirmed that when light enters a denser medium, its speed decreases but its frequency remains constant. Reason (R) is true.

3. Check if R is the correct explanation for A: Now we must see if the reason *causes* the assertion.

  • The assertion (fringes get closer) is about the fringe width (β).
  • The formula for fringe width is β = λD/d.
  • The reason states that in the medium, speed (v) decreases and frequency (f) is constant.
  • The relationship between speed, frequency, and wavelength is v = fλ.
  • If v decreases and f is constant, then wavelength λ = v/f must also decrease.
  • Since λ decreases, the fringe width β must also decrease.

The logic flows perfectly. The reason (speed decreases, frequency is constant) leads directly to the conclusion that the wavelength decreases, which in turn is the direct cause for the assertion (fringes get closer). Therefore, R is the correct explanation for A.

Derived Answer: Both (A) and (R) are true, and (R) is the correct explanation of (A).
