Build Your Skills: Electromagnetism

This guide will build your understanding from basic kinematics and electric forces to solving a JEE-level problem about an electron moving in an electric field.

Phase 1: Motion in One Dimension

The electron enters with a horizontal velocity and the electric field is vertical. This means the horizontal and vertical motions are independent.

The horizontal velocity remains constant. We can use this to find the time the electron spends between the plates.

Question 1.1: The plates are 10 cm (0.1 m) long and the horizontal velocity is 10⁶ m/s. How long does the electron spend in the electric field?
Show Solution

Time = Distance / Speed

t = 0.1 m / 10⁶ m/s = 10⁻⁷ s.

Phase 2: Force and Acceleration in an Electric Field

The electric field (E) exerts a force (F) on a charge (q) given by F = qE. By Newton's second law, this force causes an acceleration (a) given by F = ma.

Combining these, the vertical acceleration of the electron is a = qE/m.

Question 2.1: The electric field is 9.1 V/cm. Convert this to V/m. Then calculate the vertical acceleration of the electron.
Show Solution

E = 9.1 V/cm * 100 cm/m = 910 V/m.

a = (1.6 * 10⁻¹⁹ C) * (910 V/m) / (9.1 * 10⁻³¹ kg)

a = (1.6 * 10⁻¹⁹ * 100 * 9.1) / (9.1 * 10⁻³¹) = 1.6 * 10¹⁴ m/s².

Phase 3: Kinematics in the Vertical Direction

Now we can use the standard kinematic equation v = u + at to find the final vertical velocity (v_y). The initial vertical velocity (u_y) is 0.

Question 3.1: Using the time from Phase 1 and the acceleration from Phase 2, calculate the final vertical velocity of the electron as it emerges from the plates.
Show Solution

v_y = 0 + (1.6 * 10¹⁴ m/s²) * (10⁻⁷ s)

v_y = 1.6 * 10⁷ m/s.

Phase 4: The JEE Main Question

Now you have all the components to solve the final problem.

Question 26: An electron is made to enter symmetrically between two parallel and equally but oppositely charged metal plates, each of 10 cm length. The electron emerges out of the electric field region with a horizontal component of velocity 10⁶ m/s. If the magnitude of the electric field between the plates is 9.1 V/cm, then the vertical component of velocity of electron is (mass of electron = 9.1 × 10⁻³¹ kg and charge of electron = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹C)
Show Solution

Step 1: Find the time of flight.

From Phase 1, t = 10⁻⁷ s.

Step 2: Find the vertical acceleration.

From Phase 2, a = 1.6 * 10¹⁴ m/s².

Step 3: Calculate the final vertical velocity.

From Phase 3, v_y = at = (1.6 * 10¹⁴) * (10⁻⁷) = 1.6 * 10⁷ m/s.

The options are given in terms of 16 * 10^n. Let's convert our answer.

1.6 * 10⁷ = 16 * 10⁶ m/s.
