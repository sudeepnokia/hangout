Learning Guide: Gauss's Law and Electric Flux

This guide will explain the concept of electric flux and a powerful law that helps us calculate it, even in tricky situations.

Phase 1: What is Electric Flux?

Imagine an electric charge creating an invisible field around it, represented by lines pointing away from it (if it's positive). Electric Flux is a measure of how many of these field lines pass through a given surface.

Calculating this by counting lines is impossible. Luckily, a genius named Gauss came up with a much easier way.

Gauss's Law: The total electric flux (Φ) coming out of any closed surface (like a box, a sphere, or any other shape that encloses a volume) is directly proportional to the total electric charge enclosed inside that surface.

The formula is incredibly simple: Φ = Q_enclosed / ε₀

Where Q_enclosed is the net charge inside the surface, and ε₀ (epsilon-naught) is a fundamental constant called the permittivity of free space.

Warm-up Question: A charge of +10 Coulombs is placed at the exact center of a hollow cube. What is the total electric flux through the entire surface of the cube?

We don't need to know the size of the cube or anything else. We just use Gauss's Law.

The closed surface is the cube.

The charge enclosed is Q_enclosed = 10 C.

The total flux is Φ_total = 10 / ε₀.

It's that simple! The shape doesn't matter, only the charge inside.

Phase 2: A Step Up - Splitting the Flux

What if we want to know the flux through just one face of the cube, not the whole thing?

If the charge is placed symmetrically inside, the total flux will be shared equally among the identical faces.

A cube has 6 identical faces. If a charge is at the very center, the flux is split perfectly into 6 equal parts.

Question: A charge Q is placed at the exact center of a cube. What is the electric flux through just one of its faces?

Step 1: Find the total flux.

From Gauss's Law, Φ_total = Q / ε₀.

Step 2: Divide by the number of faces.

Since the charge is at the center, the setup is perfectly symmetrical. The cube has 6 faces.

Φ_one_face = Φ_total / 6 = (Q / ε₀) / 6 = Q / (6ε₀).

Phase 3: The "Aha!" Moment - Creating Symmetry

The real power of Gauss's Law is that the surface can be imaginary. We can draw any closed shape we want to make the problem easier.

What if the charge isn't inside the cube, but on an edge or a corner? We can't directly apply Gauss's Law to the single cube because the charge isn't enclosed by it.

The Trick: We build a larger, imaginary shape out of identical cubes that does enclose the charge symmetrically at its center.

Question: A charge Q is placed at the corner of a cube. What is the total flux passing through that one cube?

A single cube does not enclose the charge. But, we can imagine 8 identical cubes arranged in a 2x2x2 block. This larger block would have the charge Q at its exact center.

Now, we can apply Gauss's Law to this large 8-cube system.

Total flux through all 8 cubes is Φ_total = Q / ε₀.

Since all 8 cubes are identical and the charge is at their common center, the flux is shared equally among them.

The flux through our original, single cube is just 1/8th of the total flux.

Φ_one_cube = Φ_total / 8 = Q / (8ε₀).

Phase 4: The Summit - The JEE Main Question

Original Question: A line charge of length a/2 is kept at the center of an edge BC of a cube ABCDEFGH having edge length 'a'. If the density of line charge is λ C per unit length, then the total electric flux through all the faces of the cube will be:

This problem combines the ideas from the previous phases.

Step 1: Find the total charge (Q).

We have a line of charge, not a point charge. The charge density is λ (charge per unit length). The length of the charge is a/2.

Q = density × length = λ × (a/2) = λa / 2.

Step 2: Create a symmetrical Gaussian surface.

The line charge is sitting on one edge of our cube. This is not a symmetrical position within the cube.

Imagine placing another identical cube right next to the first one, sharing the edge BC. Now, our line charge of length a/2 is still not fully enclosed. It is on the shared face.

Let's try again. We need to enclose the charge. Imagine 4 cubes arranged around the line charge in a square. If you look down the line of the charge, you would see 4 cubes meeting at that line. This arrangement makes the line charge run along the central axis of a long 2x2 prism of cubes.

This larger shape, made of 4 cubes, now has the line charge running down its central axis. It is perfectly symmetrical.

Step 3: Apply Gauss's Law to the larger shape.

The total charge enclosed by this 4-cube system is Q_enclosed = λa / 2.

The total flux through all 4 cubes combined is:

Φ_total_4_cubes = Q_enclosed / ε₀ = (λa / 2) / ε₀ = λa / (2ε₀).

Step 4: Find the flux through the original, single cube.

Since the 4 cubes are identical and the charge is symmetrically placed among them, the total flux is shared equally.

The flux through our one cube is 1/4th of the total flux.

Φ_one_cube = Φ_total_4_cubes / 4 = (λa / (2ε₀)) / 4 = λa / (8ε₀).

Final Answer Verification

Derived Answer: The total electric flux through the cube is λa / (8ε₀).
