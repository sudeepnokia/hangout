Learning Guide: The First Law of Thermodynamics

This guide explains the fundamental law governing energy changes in a system, focusing on internal energy, heat, and work.

Phase 1: The System and Surroundings

In thermodynamics, we divide the universe into two parts:

  • The System: The part of the universe we are interested in studying (e.g., a gas in a container, a chemical reaction).
  • The Surroundings: Everything else outside the system.

An isolated system is one that cannot exchange either energy (like heat or work) or matter with its surroundings. A perfectly insulated, sealed container (like a thermos flask) is a good approximation of an isolated system.

Question: The problem describes a liquid in a "thermally insulated closed vessel". What kind of system is this, and what does that imply about the exchange of heat?

A "closed vessel" means no matter can get in or out.

"Thermally insulated" means no heat can be exchanged with the surroundings.

This describes an isolated system.

The amount of heat exchanged with the surroundings, denoted by q, must be zero.

Therefore, q = 0. This is also called an adiabatic process.

Phase 2: The Energy Balance Sheet

The First Law of Thermodynamics is a statement of the conservation of energy. It relates the change in the internal energy of a system (ΔU) to the heat (q) added to the system and the work (w) done on the system.

The First Law: ΔU = q + w

Sign Convention (from the system's perspective):

  • ΔU: The change in the total energy contained within the system.
  • q: Heat. If heat flows into the system, q is positive. If heat flows out, q is negative.
  • w: Work. If work is done on the system (e.g., compressing it), w is positive. If the system does work on the surroundings (e.g., expanding), w is negative.

Phase 3: The Summit - The JEE Main Question

Original Question: A liquid when kept inside a thermally insulated closed vessel at 25°C was mechanically stirred from outside. What will be the correct option for the following thermodynamic parameters?

Let's analyze the process step-by-step using the First Law.

Step 1: Analyze the heat (q).

The vessel is "thermally insulated". As we determined in Phase 1, this means it's an adiabatic process. No heat can enter or leave the system.

Therefore, q = 0.

Step 2: Analyze the work (w).

The liquid was "mechanically stirred from outside". This means something from the surroundings (the stirrer) is doing work on the system (the liquid). The work is being put into the system.

According to our sign convention, work done ON the system is positive.

Therefore, w > 0.

Step 3: Analyze the change in internal energy (ΔU).

Now we use the First Law of Thermodynamics: ΔU = q + w.

We substitute the values we found:

ΔU = 0 + w

Since w is a positive number, ΔU must also be a positive number.

Therefore, ΔU > 0.

A positive change in internal energy means the internal energy of the liquid has increased. This energy increase will manifest as a slight increase in the temperature of the liquid.

Conclusion: We have ΔU > 0, q = 0, and w > 0.
