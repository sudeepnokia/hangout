Build Your Skills: Conditional Probability

This guide will build your understanding from basic probability to solving a tricky JEE-level problem about dependent events.

The Scenario: A bag contains 4 White and 6 Black balls. Total balls = 10.

Phase 1: Basic Probability

Probability is the chance of something happening. The formula is: P(Event) = (Number of ways the event can happen) / (Total number of possible outcomes).

Question 1.1: If you pick one ball from the bag, what is the probability it is black?
Show Solution

Number of black balls = 6.

Total number of balls = 10.

P(Black) = 6 / 10 = 3/5.

Phase 2: When One Event Affects Another

Things get interesting when we don't put the first ball back. This is called "without replacement". The first draw changes the probabilities for the second draw.

Question 2.1: What is the probability of drawing a black ball, and then (without replacing it) drawing another black ball?
Show Solution

Probability of the first ball being black is P(1st is B) = 6/10.

After taking one black ball, the bag now has 5 black balls and 4 white balls (9 total).

So, the probability of the second ball being black is now P(2nd is B) = 5/9.

The combined probability is the product of the two steps: P(B and then B) = (6/10) * (5/9) = 30/90 = 1/3.

Phase 3: Thinking Backwards - Conditional Probability

This is the core idea. What if we know the outcome of the second event, and want to find the probability of the first? This is called Conditional Probability.

The formula is P(A | B) = P(A and B) / P(B). It reads "The probability of A happening, given that B happened, is the probability of both happening divided by the total probability of B happening."

Question 3.1: We need P(B), the total probability of the second ball being black. A black ball can be picked second in two ways: (Black, Black) or (White, Black). Calculate the total probability.
Show Solution

Path 1: P(Black and Black) = 1/3 (from Phase 2).

Path 2: P(White and Black) = P(1st is W) * P(2nd is B after W) = (4/10) * (6/9) = 24/90 = 4/15.

Total P(2nd is Black) = P(Path 1) + P(Path 2) = 1/3 + 4/15 = 5/15 + 4/15 = 9/15 = 3/5.

Phase 4: The JEE Main Question

You now have all the concepts and values needed to solve the final problem.

Question 3: Two balls are selected at random one by one without replacement from a bag containing 4 white and 6 black balls. If the probability that the first selected ball is black, given that the second selected ball is also black, is m/n, where gcd(m, n) = 1, then m + n is equal to:
Show Solution

Step 1: Identify the events.

A = The first ball is black.

B = The second ball is black.

We want to find P(A | B).

Step 2: Use the conditional probability formula.

P(A | B) = P(A and B) / P(B)

Step 3: Plug in the values from our previous phases.

P(A and B) is the probability of (Black, Black), which we found in Phase 2 is 1/3.

P(B) is the total probability of the second ball being black, which we found in Phase 3 is 3/5.

P(A | B) = (1/3) / (3/5) = (1/3) * (5/3) = 5/9.

Step 4: Solve the final part of the question.

The probability is m/n = 5/9. So, m=5 and n=9. gcd(5,9)=1.

m + n = 5 + 9 = 14.
