Learning Guide: Electron Configurations and Stability

This guide explains how to determine the electron configurations of ions and why certain configurations are especially stable.

Phase 1: The Stability of Half-Filled and Fully-Filled Orbitals

Electrons occupy orbitals (s, p, d, f). Each type of orbital can hold a certain number of electrons:

  • s-orbital: 2 electrons
  • p-orbitals: 6 electrons
  • d-orbitals: 10 electrons
  • f-orbitals: 14 electrons

A key principle in chemistry is that atoms and ions gain extra stability when their orbitals are either exactly half-filled or completely filled.

For the f-orbitals, this means configurations with f⁷ (half-filled) or f¹⁴ (fully-filled) are particularly stable. This stability is due to symmetrical distribution of electrons and maximum exchange energy.

Phase 2: Writing Configurations for Lanthanoid Ions

The Lanthanoids are the elements in the f-block, where the 4f orbital is being filled. Their general electron configuration is [Xe] 4fⁿ 5d⁰⁻¹ 6s².

When lanthanoids form ions, they lose electrons. The rule is to remove the outermost electrons first. This means electrons are removed from the 6s orbital first, then the 5d (if any), and finally from the 4f orbital.

The most common oxidation state for lanthanoids is +3, meaning they lose 3 electrons.

Question: Determine the 4f configuration for the ions given in the question: Eu²⁺, Gd³⁺, Eu³⁺, Tb³⁺, Sm²⁺.

(You would typically use a periodic table for the atomic numbers: Eu=63, Gd=64, Tb=65, Sm=62)

(A) Europium (Eu), Z=63:

  • Neutral Eu config: [Xe] 4f⁷ 6s²
  • To form Eu²⁺, remove the two 6s electrons.
  • Eu²⁺ config: [Xe] 4f⁷

(B) Gadolinium (Gd), Z=64:

  • Neutral Gd config: [Xe] 4f⁷ 5d¹ 6s² (This is an exception, gaining stability from the half-filled 4f shell)
  • To form Gd³⁺, remove the two 6s electrons and the one 5d electron.
  • Gd³⁺ config: [Xe] 4f⁷

(C) Europium (Eu), Z=63:

  • Neutral Eu config: [Xe] 4f⁷ 6s²
  • To form Eu³⁺, remove the two 6s electrons and one 4f electron.
  • Eu³⁺ config: [Xe] 4f⁶

(D) Terbium (Tb), Z=65:

  • Neutral Tb config: [Xe] 4f⁹ 6s²
  • To form Tb³⁺, remove the two 6s electrons and one 4f electron.
  • Tb³⁺ config: [Xe] 4f⁸

(E) Samarium (Sm), Z=62:

  • Neutral Sm config: [Xe] 4f⁶ 6s²
  • To form Sm²⁺, remove the two 6s electrons.
  • Sm²⁺ config: [Xe] 4f⁶
