Build Your Skills: Conic Sections

This guide will build your understanding from basic coordinate geometry to solving a JEE-level problem involving parabolas and circles.

Phase 1: Finding Where a Curve Meets the Axes

Any curve meets the y-axis where the x-coordinate is 0. It meets the x-axis where the y-coordinate is 0.

Question 1.1: A parabola is given by the equation y = x² + 2x - 3. At what point does it cross the y-axis? What are the x-coordinates of the points where it crosses the x-axis?
Show Solution

Y-intercept: Set x=0. y = 0² + 2(0) - 3 = -3. The point is (0, -3).

X-intercepts: Set y=0. x² + 2x - 3 = 0. This is a quadratic equation. We can factor it: (x+3)(x-1) = 0. The roots are x = -3 and x = 1. The points are (-3, 0) and (1, 0).

Phase 2: Defining a Circle

A circle is defined by its center and its radius. Every point on the circle is the same distance (the radius) from the center. The equation of a circle with center (h, k) and radius r is (x-h)² + (y-k)² = r².

Question 2.1: A circle has its center at (-1, -1). It passes through the point (0, -3). What is the equation of the circle?
Show Solution

The center is (h, k) = (-1, -1). To find the radius squared (r²), we can plug the point (0, -3) into the equation:

(0 - (-1))² + (-3 - (-1))² = r²

(1)² + (-2)² = r²

1 + 4 = 5 = r².

So, the equation of the circle is (x+1)² + (y+1)² = 5.

Phase 3: Finding Unknowns

Now, let's use the circle equation to find information about the parabola.

Question 3.1: A parabola y = x² + px - 3 and a circle (x+1)² + (y+1)² = 5 intersect on the x-axis. What are the coordinates of the intersection points?
Show Solution

The intersection happens on the x-axis, which means y=0. Let's plug y=0 into the circle's equation:

(x+1)² + (0+1)² = 5

(x+1)² + 1 = 5

(x+1)² = 4

x+1 = 2 or x+1 = -2.

So, x = 1 and x = -3. The intersection points are (1, 0) and (-3, 0). Since the parabola also passes through these points, these must be its x-intercepts.

Phase 4: The JEE Main Question

You have all the skills. The JEE question asks for the area of the triangle formed by the intersection points.

Question 7: Let the parabola y = x² + px - 3, meet the coordinate axes at the points P, Q and R. If the circle C with centre at (-1, -1) passes through the points P, Q and R, then the area of △PQR is:
Show Solution

Step 1: Identify the intersection points P, Q, and R.

R is the y-intercept of the parabola. Set x=0 in y = x² + px - 3 to get y=-3. So, R = (0, -3).

P and Q are the x-intercepts. The circle passes through all three points. Since the circle passes through R(0,-3) and has its center at (-1,-1), we can find its radius squared: r² = (0 - (-1))² + (-3 - (-1))² = 1² + (-2)² = 5. The circle equation is (x+1)² + (y+1)² = 5.

Since P and Q are on the x-axis (y=0) and on the circle, we set y=0 in the circle's equation: (x+1)² + (0+1)² = 5, which gives (x+1)² = 4. The solutions are x=1 and x=-3. So, P = (1, 0) and Q = (-3, 0).

Step 2: Calculate the area of △PQR.

We have a triangle with vertices (1, 0), (-3, 0), and (0, -3). We can take the segment from Q to P as the base.

Base length = |1 - (-3)| = 4.

The height is the perpendicular distance from R to the x-axis, which is |-3| = 3.

Area = 1/2 * base * height = 1/2 * 4 * 3 = 6.
