Phase 1: Representing Lines in 3D

A line in 3D can be represented by a point it passes through and a direction vector. The equation of a line is given by r = a + λb, where a is the position vector of a point on the line and b is the direction vector.

The given lines are in Cartesian form. Let's convert them to vector form.

Phase 2: The Concept of Shortest Distance

Two lines in 3D that do not intersect and are not parallel are called skew lines. The shortest distance between them is the length of the perpendicular line segment connecting them.

The direction of this shortest distance vector is given by the cross product of the direction vectors of the two lines: b₁ x b₂.

Phase 3: The Formula

The shortest distance (d) between two skew lines is the projection of the vector connecting a point on each line (a₂ - a₁) onto the shortest distance vector (b₁ x b₂).

The formula is: d = |(a₂ - a₁) . (b₁ x b₂)| / |b₁ x b₂|.

Question 3.1: Calculate the shortest distance between L₁ and L₂.
Show Solution

a₁ = i + 2j + 3k, a₂ = 2i + 4j + 5k. So, a₂ - a₁ = i + 2j + 2k.

b₁ x b₂ = -i + 2j - k. |b₁ x b₂| = √((-1)² + 2² + (-1)²) = √6.

(a₂ - a₁) . (b₁ x b₂) = (1)(-1) + (2)(2) + (2)(-1) = -1 + 4 - 2 = 1.

d = |1| / √6 = 1/√6.
