Learning Guide: Lens Combinations and Power

This guide explains how to determine the properties of a complex lens system by breaking it down into simpler parts.

Phase 1: The Lens Maker's Formula

The "power" of a lens tells us how strongly it bends light. A more powerful lens has a shorter focal length. Power (P) is the reciprocal of the focal length (f) in meters: P = 1/f.

The Lens Maker's Formula allows us to calculate the power of a thin lens based on its shape and the material it's made from.

Formula: P = 1/f = (μ - 1) * (1/R₁ - 1/R₂)

  • μ (mu): The refractive index of the lens material relative to its surroundings.
  • R₁: The radius of curvature of the first surface the light hits.
  • R₂: The radius of curvature of the second surface.

Sign Convention is CRITICAL: We measure distances from the center of the lens. Light travels from left to right.

  • If the center of curvature is to the right of the surface, R is positive.
  • If the center of curvature is to the left of the surface, R is negative.
Warm-up Question: A standard biconvex lens has two surfaces, both with radius R. The first surface is convex (center to the right), and the second is concave (center to the left). What is its power if it's made of glass (μ_g) and is in air (μ_air = 1)?

First surface (left): Convex, center is to the right, so R₁ = +R.

Second surface (right): Concave, center is to the left, so R₂ = -R.

The relative refractive index is μ = μ_g / μ_air = μ_g.

P = (μ_g - 1) * (1/R - 1/(-R))

P = (μ_g - 1) * (1/R + 1/R) = (μ_g - 1) * (2/R)

Phase 2: Combining Powers

What happens when you place several thin lenses right next to each other? The total power of the combination is simply the sum of the individual powers.

Formula: P_total = P₁ + P₂ + P₃ + ...

This is a very powerful tool. It allows us to treat a complex stack of lenses as a single equivalent lens.

Question: The problem shows a container that creates three lenses stacked together: a water lens, a glass lens, and another water lens. How would you find the total power of this system?

We would calculate the power of each of the three lenses individually using the Lens Maker's Formula, and then add them up.

P_total = P_water1 + P_glass + P_water2
