Build Your Skills: Optics

This guide will build your understanding from the basics of lenses and mirrors to solving a JEE-level problem involving a silvered lens.

Phase 1: The Lensmaker's Formula

The power (P) of a lens, which is the reciprocal of its focal length (f), is given by the Lensmaker's formula:

P_lens = 1/f_lens = (μ - 1) * (1/R₁ - 1/R₂)

where μ is the refractive index, and R₁ and R₂ are the radii of curvature of the two surfaces.

Question 1.1: For the given convex lens, both sides have the same radius of curvature R. Using the sign convention (light travels left to right), R₁ is positive and R₂ is negative. Find the power of the lens.
Show Solution

R₁ = R and R₂ = -R.

P_lens = (μ - 1) * (1/R - 1/(-R)) = (μ - 1) * (2/R).

Phase 2: The Mirror Formula

The power of a spherical mirror is given by P_mirror = -1/f_mirror. The focal length of a spherical mirror is half its radius of curvature, f = R/2.

Question 2.1: The back surface of the lens is polished, so it acts as a concave mirror. Its radius of curvature is R₂ = -R. What is the power of this mirror?
Show Solution

The focal length of the mirror is f_mirror = R₂/2 = -R/2.

P_mirror = -1/f_mirror = -1/(-R/2) = 2/R.

Phase 3: The Equivalent Power of a Silvered Lens

When a lens is silvered on one side, it acts as a combination of a lens and a mirror. A light ray passes through the lens, reflects off the mirror, and passes back through the lens. The equivalent power of the system is:

P_eq = P_lens + P_mirror + P_lens = 2*P_lens + P_mirror.

Question 3.1: Calculate the equivalent power of the silvered lens system.
Show Solution

P_eq = 2 * [(μ - 1) * (2/R)] + 2/R

P_eq = (4(μ-1)/R) + 2/R = (4μ - 4 + 2)/R = (4μ - 2)/R.

Phase 4: The JEE Main Question

The problem states that the image is formed on the object itself. This happens when the object is placed at the center of curvature of the equivalent optical element. For a mirror, this means the object is at a distance of 2f_eq from the mirror, where f_eq is the equivalent focal length.

Question 32: Given is a thin convex lens of glass (refractive index μ) and each side having radius of curvature R. One side is polished for complete reflection. At what distance from the lens, an object be placed on the optic axis so that the image gets formed on the object itself?
Show Solution

Step 1: Find the equivalent focal length.

The equivalent system acts like a mirror. The equivalent power is P_eq = (4μ - 2)/R.

The equivalent focal length is f_eq = -1/P_eq = -R / (4μ - 2).

Step 2: Find the object distance.

For the image to form on the object, the object must be placed at the center of curvature of the equivalent mirror, which is at a distance of 2 * |f_eq| from the mirror.

Object distance u = 2 * |-R / (4μ - 2)| = 2R / (4μ - 2) = R / (2μ - 1).
