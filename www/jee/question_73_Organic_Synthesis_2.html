Learning Guide: Aromatic Synthesis via Diazonium Salts

This guide breaks down a four-step synthesis involving the reduction of a nitro group, diazotization, and two different substitution reactions.

Phase 1: The Reactions

Let's understand the purpose of each step. The starting material is nitrobenzene.

  1. (i) Sn + HCl: This is the standard reduction of a nitro group (-NO₂) to a primary amine (-NH₂).
  2. (ii) NaNO₂, HCl, 0°C: This is the diazotization reaction, which converts the primary amine (-NH₂) into a diazonium salt (-N₂⁺Cl⁻).
  3. (iii) Cu₂Cl₂: This reagent is used in the Sandmeyer reaction. It replaces the diazonium group (-N₂⁺) with a chlorine atom (-Cl). The source of the chloride is the HCl from the previous step.
  4. (iv) NaI, Ether: This is a nucleophilic substitution reaction. The iodide ion (I⁻) from sodium iodide is a strong nucleophile that will replace the chlorine atom on the benzene ring. This is a variation of the Finkelstein reaction, applied to an aryl halide. While typically difficult, the reaction is driven by the precipitation of NaCl in a non-polar solvent like ether.

Phase 2: Tracing the Molecule

Original Question: Consider the following sequence of reactions:

Nitrobenzene → (i) Sn + HCl → (ii) NaNO₂, HCl, 0°C → (iii) Cu₂Cl₂ → (iv) NaI, Ether → Product (A)

Molar mass of the product formed (A) is ___ gmol⁻¹.

Let's trace the transformation of the molecule through each step.

Starting Material: Nitrobenzene (C₆H₅NO₂)

Step (i): Reduction (Sn + HCl)

The nitro group is reduced to an amine group.

Product of Step (i): Aniline (C₆H₅NH₂)

Step (ii): Diazotization (NaNO₂, HCl)

The amine group is converted to a diazonium salt.

Product of Step (ii): Benzenediazonium chloride (C₆H₅N₂⁺Cl⁻)

Step (iii): Sandmeyer Reaction (Cu₂Cl₂)

The diazonium group is replaced by a chlorine atom.

Product of Step (iii): Chlorobenzene (C₆H₅Cl)

Step (iv): Finkelstein-like Reaction (NaI, Ether)

The chlorine atom is replaced by an iodine atom.

Final Product (A): Iodobenzene (C₆H₅I)

Step 5: Calculate the Molar Mass of the Final Product.

The final product is Iodobenzene (C₆H₅I).

We need the atomic masses:

  • Carbon (C): ~12.0 g/mol
  • Hydrogen (H): ~1.0 g/mol
  • Iodine (I): ~127.0 g/mol

Molar Mass = (6 × 12.0) + (5 × 1.0) + (1 × 127.0)

Molar Mass = 72 + 5 + 127 = 204 g/mol.
