Phase 1: What is a Forward-Biased Diode?

A diode is an electronic component that allows current to flow easily in one direction but not the other. The arrow in the diode symbol shows the direction of easy current flow.

A diode is forward-biased when the voltage at the p-side (the flat base of the triangle) is higher than the voltage at the n-side (the tip of the triangle). When a diode is forward-biased, it acts like a closed switch and allows current to flow.

If the voltage at the p-side is lower than the n-side, it is reverse-biased and acts like an open switch, blocking current.
