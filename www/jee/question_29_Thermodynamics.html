Build Your Skills: Thermodynamics

This guide will build your understanding from basic heat calculations to solving a multi-step JEE-level thermodynamics problem.

Phase 1: Heat Required for Temperature Change (Sensible Heat)

The amount of heat (Q) required to change the temperature of a substance is given by the formula Q = mcΔT, where m is the mass, c is the specific heat capacity, and ΔT is the change in temperature.

Question 1.1: Calculate the heat required to raise the temperature of 10⁻³ kg of ice from -10°C to 0°C. (Specific heat of ice = 2100 J/kgK)
Show Solution

Q₁ = (10⁻³ kg) * (2100 J/kgK) * (0 - (-10))K = 10⁻³ * 2100 * 10 = 21 J.

Phase 2: The Heat of Transformation

When a substance changes phase (e.g., solid to liquid or liquid to gas), it absorbs or releases heat without changing its temperature. This is called latent heat. The formula is Q = mL, where L is the specific latent heat.

Question 2.1: Calculate the heat required to melt 10⁻³ kg of ice at 0°C into water at 0°C. (Latent heat of ice = 3.35 x 10⁵ J/kg)
Show Solution

Q₂ = (10⁻³ kg) * (3.35 * 10⁵ J/kg) = 335 J.

Phase 3: Chaining the Calculations

The total heat required for the entire process is the sum of the heat required for each individual step.

The steps are:

  1. Heat ice from -10°C to 0°C (Q₁)
  2. Melt ice to water at 0°C (Q₂)
  3. Heat water from 0°C to 100°C (Q₃)
  4. Boil water to steam at 100°C (Q₄)
  5. Heat steam from 100°C to 110°C (Q₅)
Question 3.1: Calculate Q₃, Q₄, and Q₅.
Show Solution

Q₃ (Heat water): (10⁻³ kg) * (4180 J/kgK) * (100 - 0)K = 418 J.

Q₄ (Boil water): (10⁻³ kg) * (2.25 * 10⁶ J/kg) = 2250 J.

Q₅ (Heat steam): (10⁻³ kg) * (1920 J/kgK) * (110 - 100)K = 19.2 J.

Phase 4: The JEE Main Question

Now we can solve the full problem by summing the heat from all the steps.

Question 29: An amount of ice of mass 10⁻³ kg and temperature -10°C is transformed to vapour of temperature 110°C by applying heat. The total amount of work required for this conversion is, (Take, specific heat of ice = 2100Jkg⁻¹K⁻¹, specific heat of water = 4180Jkg⁻¹K⁻¹, specific heat of steam = 1920Jkg⁻¹K⁻¹, Latent heat of ice = 3.35 × 10⁵Jkg⁻¹ and Latent heat of steam = 2.25 x 10⁶ Jkg⁻¹)
Show Solution

Step 1: Sum the heat required for each step.

Total Heat = Q₁ + Q₂ + Q₃ + Q₄ + Q₅

Total Heat = 21 J + 335 J + 418 J + 2250 J + 19.2 J = 3043.2 J.

The question asks for the total work required, which in this context is the total heat energy supplied.
