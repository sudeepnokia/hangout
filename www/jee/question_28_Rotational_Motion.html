Build Your Skills: Rotational Motion

This guide will build your understanding from the basics of moment of inertia to solving a JEE-level problem involving a composite body.

Phase 1: Moment of Inertia and the Parallel Axis Theorem

Moment of Inertia (I) is the rotational equivalent of mass. For a uniform disc of mass M and radius R, the moment of inertia about an axis through its center and perpendicular to its plane is I_cm = (1/2)MR².

The Parallel Axis Theorem helps us find the moment of inertia about an axis parallel to the one through the center of mass. The formula is I = I_cm + md², where m is the mass and d is the distance between the two parallel axes.

Phase 2: Dealing with Removed Parts

The problem involves removing a smaller disc from a larger one. We can think of the remaining object as the original large disc plus a "negative mass" disc where the part was removed.

The moment of inertia of the remaining part is: I_rem = I_total - I_removed.

Question 2.1: The original disc has mass M and radius R. What is its moment of inertia about the given axis (which passes through its center)?
Show Solution

I_total = (1/2)MR².

Phase 3: Finding the Mass and Moment of Inertia of the Removed Disc

First, we need the mass of the small disc that was removed. We assume the original disc is uniform, so its mass is proportional to its area (σ = M/Area). The area of a disc is πr².

Question 3.1: The removed disc has a radius of R/2. What is its mass (m_removed) in terms of M?
Show Solution

The surface density is σ = M / (πR²).

The area of the removed disc is π(R/2)² = πR²/4.

m_removed = σ * Area_removed = (M/πR²) * (πR²/4) = M/4.

Question 3.2: The center of the removed disc is at a distance of R/2 from the axis of rotation. What is its moment of inertia (I_removed) about the axis of rotation of the large disc?
Show Solution

We need to use the Parallel Axis Theorem.

First, the moment of inertia of the small disc about its own center is I_cm_removed = (1/2) * m_removed * (R/2)² = (1/2) * (M/4) * (R²/4) = MR²/32.

The distance between the axis of rotation and the center of the removed disc is d = R/2.

I_removed = I_cm_removed + m_removed * d²

I_removed = MR²/32 + (M/4) * (R/2)² = MR²/32 + MR²/16 = 3MR²/32.

Phase 4: The JEE Main Question

Now we can find the moment of inertia of the remaining part.

Question 28: A uniform circular disc of radius 'R' and mass 'M' is rotating about an axis perpendicular to its plane and passing through its centre. A small circular part of radius R/2 is removed from the original disc as shown in the figure. Find the moment of inertia of the remaining part of the original disc about the axis as given above.
Show Solution

Step 1: Use the principle of superposition.

I_remaining = I_total - I_removed.

Step 2: Substitute the values from the previous phases.

I_total = (1/2)MR².

I_removed = 3MR²/32.

Step 3: Calculate the final answer.

I_remaining = (1/2)MR² - 3MR²/32 = (16/32)MR² - 3MR²/32 = 13MR²/32.
