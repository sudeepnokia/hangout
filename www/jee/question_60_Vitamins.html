Learning Guide: Identifying Vitamins by Chemical Name

This guide helps you connect the common names of vitamins to their chemical structures and names, focusing on carboxylic acids.

Phase 1: What is a Vitamin?

Vitamins are organic molecules that are essential micronutrients which an organism needs in small quantities for the proper functioning of its metabolism. Essential nutrients cannot be synthesized in the organism, either at all or not in sufficient quantities, and therefore must be obtained through the diet.

Vitamins are classified by their biological and chemical activity, not their structure. Each "vitamin" refers to a number of vitamer compounds that all show the biological activity associated with a particular vitamin. For example, "vitamin A" includes retinal, retinol, and several other carotenoids.

Many vitamins have common chemical names, and it's important to be able to recognize them.

Phase 2: A Look at the Acids

The question asks which of the given acids is a vitamin. Let's look at the structure and function of each one.

  • Adipic Acid: HOOC-(CH₂)₄-COOH. This is a dicarboxylic acid. It is primarily used in industry as a precursor for the production of nylon. It is not a vitamin.
  • Ascorbic Acid: This is a water-soluble compound with the formula C₆H₈O₆. It is found in citrus fruits and many other vegetables. Its deficiency causes the disease scurvy. Ascorbic acid is the chemical name for Vitamin C.
  • Saccharic Acid: HOOC-(CHOH)₄-COOH. This is a dicarboxylic acid derived from the oxidation of a sugar like glucose. It is not a vitamin.
  • Aspartic Acid: This is an α-amino acid with the formula HOOC-CH(NH₂)-CH₂-COOH. It is one of the 20 proteinogenic amino acids. While it is an essential nutrient (as a building block for proteins), it is classified as an amino acid, not a vitamin. The body can synthesize it, so it's a non-essential amino acid.

Phase 3: The Summit - The JEE Main Question

Original Question: Which of the following acids is a vitamin?

  1. Adipic acid
  2. Ascorbic acid
  3. Saccharic acid
  4. Aspartic acid

Based on our analysis in Phase 2:

  • Adipic acid is an industrial chemical.
  • Saccharic acid is a sugar derivative.
  • Aspartic acid is an amino acid.
  • Ascorbic acid is the chemical name for Vitamin C.

Therefore, ascorbic acid is the correct answer.

Final Answer Verification

Derived Answer: Ascorbic acid is a vitamin (Vitamin C).
