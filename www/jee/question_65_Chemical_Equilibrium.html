Learning Guide: Gaseous Equilibrium and Kp

This guide explains how to solve for the equilibrium constant, Kp, using partial pressures and an ICE table.

Phase 1: The Equilibrium Constant, Kp

For a reversible reaction involving gases, we can describe the state of equilibrium using the equilibrium constant, Kp. Kp is defined based on the partial pressures of the gases at equilibrium.

For a general reaction: aA(g) + bB(g) ↔ cC(g) + dD(g)

The expression for Kp is:

Kp = (P_C)ᶜ * (P_D)ᵈ / (P_A)ᵃ * (P_B)ᵇ

Where P_A, P_B, P_C, P_D are the partial pressures of the gases at equilibrium. The exponents are the stoichiometric coefficients from the balanced equation.

Important: Pure solids and pure liquids do not appear in the Kp expression because their "pressure" or concentration is considered constant.

Warm-up Question: Write the Kp expression for the reaction: CO₂(g) + C(s) ↔ 2CO(g).

The products are on top, reactants on the bottom.

Product: CO(g), coefficient is 2.

Reactants: CO₂(g), coefficient is 1. C(s) is a solid, so it is not included in the Kp expression.

Kp = (P_CO)² / (P_CO₂)

Phase 2: Dalton's Law

Dalton's Law of Partial Pressures states that the total pressure of a mixture of gases is equal to the sum of the partial pressures of the individual gases.

P_total = P_A + P_B + P_C + ...

This is crucial for relating the pressures of the gases at equilibrium to the total pressure of the system, which is often what we can measure.

Phase 3: The ICE Table Method

The ICE (Initial, Change, Equilibrium) table is a systematic way to solve equilibrium problems. It helps you track the partial pressures of all reactants and products.

  1. I (Initial): Write down the initial partial pressures of all species.
  2. C (Change): Define a variable (like 'x') to represent the change in pressure as the reaction moves towards equilibrium. The change for each species is determined by the stoichiometry of the balanced equation. Reactants decrease (-x), products increase (+x).
  3. E (Equilibrium): Add the Initial and Change rows to get expressions for the partial pressures at equilibrium.

Original Question: A vessel at 1000 K contains CO₂ with a pressure of 0.5 atm. Some of the CO₂ is converted into CO on addition of graphite. If total pressure at equilibrium is 0.8 atm, then Kp is:

Reaction: CO₂(g) + C(s) ↔ 2CO(g)

Let's set up an ICE table for the partial pressures.

CO₂(g) C(s) 2CO(g)
I (Initial) 0.5 atm (solid) 0 atm
C (Change) -x (solid) +2x
E (Equilibrium) 0.5 - x (solid) 2x

Step 1: Use the total pressure to find x.

At equilibrium, the total pressure is the sum of the partial pressures of the gases:

P_total = P_CO₂ + P_CO

We are given P_total = 0.8 atm.

0.8 = (0.5 - x) + (2x)

0.8 = 0.5 + x

x = 0.8 - 0.5 = 0.3 atm

Step 2: Calculate the equilibrium partial pressures.

Now that we have x, we can find the pressure of each gas at equilibrium:

P_CO₂ = 0.5 - x = 0.5 - 0.3 = 0.2 atm

P_CO = 2x = 2 * 0.3 = 0.6 atm

Step 3: Calculate Kp.

Using the expression from Phase 1:

Kp = (P_CO)² / (P_CO₂)

Kp = (0.6)² / (0.2)

Kp = 0.36 / 0.2 = 3.6 / 2 = 1.8 atm
