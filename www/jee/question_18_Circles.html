Build Your Skills: Circles

This guide will build your understanding from basic circle properties to solving a JEE-level problem involving the intersection of two circles.

Phase 1: Understanding the First Circle (C)

The problem describes a circle C with radius 2 that lies in the second quadrant and touches both coordinate axes.

Question 1.1: If a circle in the second quadrant touches both the x-axis and the y-axis, what is the relationship between the coordinates of its center (h, k) and its radius R? What is the center of circle C?
Show Solution

In the second quadrant, x is negative and y is positive. For the circle to touch both axes, the absolute value of its x-coordinate must equal its radius, and its y-coordinate must also equal its radius.

So, h = -R and k = R.

Given that the radius R of circle C is 2, its center must be at (-2, 2).

Phase 2: The Intersection Condition

We have a second circle with center at (2, 5) and an unknown radius r. The two circles intersect at exactly two points.

Let the centers be C₁(-2, 2) and C₂(2, 5), and the radii be R=2 and r. The distance between the centers (d) is a key value.

Question 2.1: Calculate the distance (d) between the centers C₁ and C₂.
Show Solution

Using the distance formula: d = √((x₂-x₁)² + (y₂-y₁)²)`

d = √((2 - (-2))² + (5 - 2)²) = √(4² + 3²) = √(16 + 9) = √25 = 5.

Question 2.2: For two circles to intersect at two distinct points, the distance between their centers (d) must be less than the sum of their radii (R+r) and greater than the absolute difference of their radii |R-r|.
Show Solution

The condition is: |R - r| < d < R + r.

Phase 3: Finding the Range of the Unknown Radius

Now we use the condition from Phase 2 to find the possible values for r.

Question 3.1: Substitute the known values (d=5, R=2) into the inequality and solve for r.
Show Solution

We have two inequalities:

1) d < R + r => 5 < 2 + r => r > 3.

2) |R - r| < d => |2 - r| < 5. This means -5 < 2 - r < 5.

- -5 < 2 - r => r < 7.

- 2 - r < 5 => -3 < r => r > -3 (radius must be positive anyway).

Combining all conditions: we need r > 3 AND r < 7. So, the interval for r is (3, 7).

Phase 4: The JEE Main Question

Now you have all the components to solve the final problem.

Question 18: A circle C of radius 2 lies in the second quadrant and touches both the coordinate axes. Let r be the radius of a circle that has centre at the point (2,5) and intersects the circle C at exactly two points. If the set of all possible values of r is the interval (α, β), then 3β – 2α is equal to:
Show Solution

Step 1: Find the interval (α, β).

From our work in the previous phases, we found that the possible values for r are in the interval (3, 7).

So, α = 3 and β = 7.

Step 2: Calculate the final expression.

We need to find 3β - 2α.

3(7) - 2(3) = 21 - 6 = 15.
