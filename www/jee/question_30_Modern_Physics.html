Phase 1: The Bohr Model and de Broglie Wavelength

In the Bohr model of the hydrogen atom, the angular momentum of an electron in an orbit is quantized: mvr = nh/(2π), where n is the principal quantum number (n=1, 2, 3...).

The de Broglie wavelength (λ) of a particle is given by λ = h/p = h/(mv), where p is the momentum.

Question 1.1: Combine these two equations to find a relationship between the de Broglie wavelength (λ) and the radius of the orbit (r).
Show Solution

From the de Broglie equation, mv = h/λ.

Substitute this into the Bohr model equation:

(h/λ) * r = nh/(2π)

2πr = nλ. This is a very important relationship!

Phase 2: How the Radius Depends on n

The radius of the n-th orbit in the Bohr model is proportional to the square of the principal quantum number: r_n ∝ n².

This means that the ratio of two radii is equal to the ratio of the squares of their quantum numbers: r₂ / r₁ = (n₂ / n₁)².

Phase 3: The JEE Main Question

Now we can combine these concepts to solve the problem.

Question 30: An electron in the ground state of the hydrogen atom has the orbital radius of 5.3 × 10⁻¹¹ m while that for the electron in third excited state is 8.48 × 10⁻¹⁰ m. The ratio of the de Broglie wavelengths of electron in the excited state to that in the ground state is:
Show Solution

Step 1: Identify the quantum numbers.

Ground state corresponds to n=1.

Third excited state means the electron has jumped 3 levels above the ground state, so it is in the n=4 state.

Let the ground state be state 1 (n₁=1) and the third excited state be state 2 (n₂=4).

Step 2: Use the relationship from Phase 1.

We have 2πr₁ = n₁λ₁ and 2πr₂ = n₂λ₂.

We want to find the ratio λ₂ / λ₁.

λ₂ / λ₁ = (2πr₂ / n₂) / (2πr₁ / n₁) = (r₂ / r₁) * (n₁ / n₂).

Step 3: Use the relationship from Phase 2.

We know r₂ / r₁ = (n₂ / n₁)².

Substitute this into our ratio equation:

λ₂ / λ₁ = (n₂ / n₁)² * (n₁ / n₂) = n₂ / n₁.

Step 4: Calculate the final ratio.

λ₂ / λ₁ = 4 / 1 = 4.

Note that the given radii values are not needed to solve the problem, they are there to confirm the r ∝ n² relationship if you wish to check it.
