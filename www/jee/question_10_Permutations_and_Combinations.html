Build Your Skills: Permutations and Combinations

This guide will build your understanding from basic counting principles to solving a JEE-level problem involving combinations and arrangements.

Phase 1: Choosing vs. Arranging

In these problems, it's important to know the difference between combinations (order doesn't matter) and permutations (order matters).

The problem states that the chosen letters are "arranged in alphabetical order". This is a key phrase. It means that once we choose a set of 5 letters, there is only ONE way to arrange them. For example, if we choose the letters {E, A, C, B, D}, the only valid arrangement is (A, B, C, D, E).

This means we only need to worry about choosing the letters (combinations), not arranging them.

Question 1.1: The formula for choosing k items from a set of n items is given by nCk or "n choose k". How many ways can you choose 2 letters from the set {A, B, C, D}?
Show Solution

The formula is nCk = n! / (k! * (n-k)!).

Here, n=4 and k=2.

4C2 = 4! / (2! * 2!) = (4*3*2*1) / ((2*1)*(2*1)) = 24 / 4 = 6.

The 6 combinations are: {A,B}, {A,C}, {A,D}, {B,C}, {B,D}, {C,D}.

Phase 2: Handling Constraints

The problem has a major constraint: the middle letter of the 5 chosen letters must be 'M'.

If we have 5 letters in alphabetical order (L₁, L₂, L₃, L₄, L₅), the middle letter is L₃. So, we know for sure that L₃ = M.

Question 2.1: If the middle letter must be M, where must the other 4 letters come from in the alphabet?
Show Solution

Since the letters are in alphabetical order, the first two letters (L₁ and L₂) must come from the letters before M.

The last two letters (L₄ and L₅) must come from the letters after M.

Phase 3: Solving the Sub-problems

Now we can treat this as two separate, smaller problems.

The English alphabet has 26 letters. The letter M is the 13th letter.

Question 3.1: How many letters are there before M in the alphabet? How many ways can we choose the first two letters (L₁ and L₂) from this group?
Show Solution

There are 12 letters before M (A, B, C, ..., L).

We need to choose 2 letters from these 12. The number of ways is 12C2.

12C2 = 12! / (2! * 10!) = (12 * 11) / 2 = 66 ways.

Question 3.2: How many letters are there after M in the alphabet? How many ways can we choose the last two letters (L₄ and L₅) from this group?
Show Solution

There are 13 letters after M (N, O, P, ..., Z).

We need to choose 2 letters from these 13. The number of ways is 13C2.

13C2 = 13! / (2! * 11!) = (13 * 12) / 2 = 78 ways.

Phase 4: The JEE Main Question

To get the total number of ways, we multiply the number of ways for each independent choice.

Question 10: From all the English alphabets, five letters are chosen and are arranged in alphabetical order. The total number of ways, in which the middle letter is 'M', is:
Show Solution

Step 1: Fix the middle letter.

The middle letter is M. This is 1 choice.

Step 2: Choose the two letters before M.

There are 12 letters before M. We need to choose 2. Number of ways = 12C2 = 66.

Step 3: Choose the two letters after M.

There are 13 letters after M. We need to choose 2. Number of ways = 13C2 = 78.

Step 4: Find the total number of ways.

Total ways = (Ways to choose letters before M) * (Ways to choose M) * (Ways to choose letters after M)

Total ways = 66 * 1 * 78 = 5148.
