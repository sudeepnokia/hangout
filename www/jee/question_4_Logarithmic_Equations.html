Build Your Skills: Logarithmic Equations

This guide will build your understanding from basic exponent rules to solving a complex logarithmic equation from the JEE Main exam.

Phase 1: What is a Logarithm?

A logarithm is the inverse of an exponent. The natural logarithm (ln) is the inverse of the number e (approx 2.718).

This means that ln(e^a) = a and e^(ln(b)) = b. They cancel each other out.

A key rule we will use is: ln(x^k) = k * ln(x). The exponent can be brought down in front.

Question 1.1: How can you simplify the equation e^y = x^8 to get y by itself?
Show Solution

To get rid of the 'e' on the left, we take the natural log (ln) of both sides:

ln(e^y) = ln(x^8)

The left side simplifies to just 'y'. On the right side, we can bring the exponent down:

y = 8 * ln(x)

Phase 2: Finding Hidden Quadratic Equations

Sometimes, a complex equation is just a simple quadratic equation (ax² + bx + c = 0) in disguise. The key is to find what to substitute.

Question 2.1: Look at the equation 5(lnx)² - 8(lnx) + 3 = 0. Does this look like a quadratic equation? What could you substitute to make it look like one?
Show Solution

Yes, it does! Notice that the term lnx appears squared and also by itself.

If we let y = lnx, the equation becomes:

5y² - 8y + 3 = 0

This is a standard quadratic equation that we can solve for y.

Phase 3: Solving and Reversing the Substitution

Now we solve the quadratic and then go back to our original variable.

Question 3.1: Solve 5y² - 8y + 3 = 0 for y. Then, since we know y = lnx, find the corresponding values for x.
Show Solution

We can factor the quadratic: (5y - 3)(y - 1) = 0.

The solutions for y are y₁ = 1 and y₂ = 3/5.

Now we reverse the substitution:

If ln(x₁) = 1, then x₁ = e¹ = e.

If ln(x₂) = 3/5, then x₂ = e^(3/5).

So the two solutions for x are e and e^(3/5).

Phase 4: The JEE Main Question

You have all the skills. The JEE question combines these steps. Try it yourself first.

Question 4: The product of all solutions of the equation e^(5(logₑx)²+3) = x⁸, x > 0, is:
Show Solution

Step 1: Simplify the equation by taking the natural log of both sides.

ln(e^(5(lnx)²+3)) = ln(x⁸)

5(lnx)² + 3 = 8lnx

Step 2: Rearrange into a quadratic equation.

5(lnx)² - 8lnx + 3 = 0

Step 3: Substitute and solve for the roots.

Let y = lnx. The equation is 5y² - 8y + 3 = 0. We already solved this in Phase 3. The solutions are y₁ = 1 and y₂ = 3/5.

Step 4: Find the solutions for x.

The solutions for x are x₁ = e¹ and x₂ = e^(3/5).

Step 5: Find the product of the solutions.

Product = x₁ * x₂ = e¹ * e^(3/5) = e^(1 + 3/5) = e^(8/5).
