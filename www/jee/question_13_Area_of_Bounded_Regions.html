Build Your Skills: Area Between Curves

This guide will build your understanding from identifying shapes to finding the area of a complex region defined by a circle and a parabola.

Phase 1: Understanding the Shapes and Where They Meet

First, let's understand the boundaries of our region.

The circle is (x - 2√3)² + y² = 12. From the standard form (x-h)²+(y-k)²=r², we see its center is (2√3, 0) and its radius is r = √12 = 2√3.

The parabola is y² = 2√3x. Its vertex is at (0,0) and it opens to the right.

Question 1.1: Find the (x, y) coordinates where these two curves intersect.
Show Solution

We can substitute y² = 2√3x from the parabola equation into the circle equation:

(x - 2√3)² + (2√3x) = 12

x² - 4√3x + 12 + 2√3x = 12

x² - 2√3x = 0

x(x - 2√3) = 0. So they intersect at x=0 and x=2√3.

When x=0, y=0. Point is (0,0).

When x=2√3, y² = 2√3(2√3) = 12, so y = ±√12 = ±2√3. Points are (2√3, 2√3) and (2√3, -2√3).

Key Insight: The intersection line x=2√3 is a vertical line passing through the center of the circle!

Phase 2: A Picture of the Area

The problem asks for the area "inside the circle" and "outside the parabola". This means we want the part of the circle that the parabola does not cover.

Imagine the circle. The parabola starts at the origin and cuts a "bite" out of the left side of the circle. The area we want is everything else.

The easiest way to calculate this is: Area = (Total Area of Circle) - (Area of the bite). The "bite" is the area inside the circle AND inside the parabola.

Because the shapes are symmetric about the x-axis, we can find the area of the top half of the bite (in the first quadrant) and double it.

Question 2.1: Write an expression for the area in the first quadrant as the area under the "upper" curve minus the area under the "lower" curve.
Show Solution

In the first quadrant, the region is bounded above by the circle y = √(12 - (x-2√3)²) and below by the parabola y = √(2√3x).

The area of the region we want is: Area = ∫ (y_circle - y_parabola) dx from x=0 to x=2√3.

Phase 3: Finding the Areas Using Geometry and Calculus

Let's calculate the two parts of our integral separately.

Question 3.1: Calculate the area under the parabola in the first quadrant: ∫(from 0 to 2√3) √(2√3x) dx.
Show Solution

∫(from 0 to 2√3) (2√3)^(1/2) * x^(1/2) dx

= (2√3)^(1/2) * [x^(3/2) / (3/2)] (from 0 to 2√3)

= (2√3)^(1/2) * (2/3) * (2√3)^(3/2) = (2/3) * (2√3)² = (2/3) * 12 = 8.

Question 3.2: Calculate the area under the circle in the first quadrant: ∫(from 0 to 2√3) √(12 - (x-2√3)²) dx. (Hint: This integral represents the area of a simple geometric shape!)
Show Solution

The function y = √(12 - (x-2√3)²) is the top half of the circle. The integral from x=0 to x=2√3 covers the area from the circle's leftmost point to its center.

This is exactly the area of a quarter of the circle.

The full circle's area is πr² = π(√12)² = 12π.

The area of the quarter circle is 12π / 4 = 3π.

Phase 4: The JEE Main Question

Now, combine your results to find the final answer. A common convention for these problems is to ask for the area in the first quadrant unless specified otherwise, which the options confirm.

Question 13: The area of the region, inside the circle (x – 2√3)² + y² = 12 and outside the parabola y² = 2√3x is:
Show Solution

Step 1: Define the area to be calculated.

We are looking for the area in the first quadrant bounded by the circle and the parabola. This is the area under the circle minus the area under the parabola over the interval [0, 2√3].

Area = (Area under circle in Q1) - (Area under parabola in Q1)

Step 2: Use the values calculated in the previous phases.

Area under circle in Q1 = 3π.

Area under parabola in Q1 = 8.

Step 3: Calculate the final area.

Area = 3π - 8.
