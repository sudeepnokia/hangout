Learning Guide: Acidity of Alkynes and Gas Stoichiometry

This guide explains why certain alkynes are acidic and how to relate chemical reactions to gas volumes.

Phase 1: Acidity of Terminal Alkynes

An alkyne is a hydrocarbon with a carbon-carbon triple bond (C≡C).

A terminal alkyne is an alkyne where the triple bond is at the end of the carbon chain, meaning one of the triply-bonded carbons is attached to a hydrogen atom (R-C≡C-H).

This hydrogen atom is unusually acidic compared to hydrogens on double or single bonds. This is because the carbon atom of the C-H bond is sp hybridized. The high s-character (50%) of the sp orbital pulls the electron density away from the hydrogen, making it easier to remove as a proton (H⁺).

Because of this acidity, terminal alkynes can react with very strong bases, like sodium (Na) or sodamide (NaNH₂), to lose their proton.

Statement I: One mole of propyne reacts with excess of sodium to liberate half a mole of H₂ gas.

Question: Analyze the reaction of propyne with sodium. Is Statement I correct?

Propyne has the structure CH₃-C≡C-H. It is a terminal alkyne because of the hydrogen attached to the triple bond.

The reaction with sodium (a strong base/reducing agent) is an acid-base reaction where the acidic hydrogen is replaced.

The balanced chemical equation is:

2 CH₃-C≡C-H + 2 Na → 2 CH₃-C≡C⁻Na⁺ + H₂(↑)

Let's look at the stoichiometry (the mole ratios):

2 moles of propyne react to produce 1 mole of hydrogen gas (H₂).

Therefore, if we start with 1 mole of propyne, it will produce 0.5 moles (half a mole) of H₂ gas.

The statement says it liberates half a mole of H₂ gas. This is correct. Statement I is correct.

Phase 2: A Step Up - The Ideal Gas Law at STP

Avogadro's Law states that equal volumes of all ideal gases, at the same temperature and pressure, have the same number of molecules.

A very useful consequence of this is the concept of molar volume. At Standard Temperature and Pressure (STP), which is 0°C (273.15 K) and 1 atm pressure:

1 mole of any ideal gas occupies a volume of 22.4 Liters (L) or 22400 milliliters (mL).

Statement II: Four g of propyne reacts with NaNH₂ to liberate NH₃ gas which occupies 224 mL at STP.

Question: Analyze this reaction and the volume of gas produced. Is Statement II correct?

Step 1: Write the balanced chemical equation.

Sodamide (NaNH₂) is a very strong base. The acidic proton of propyne will react with the amide ion (NH₂⁻) to form ammonia (NH₃).

CH₃-C≡C-H + NaNH₂ → CH₃-C≡C⁻Na⁺ + NH₃(↑)

The stoichiometry is simple: 1 mole of propyne produces 1 mole of ammonia gas.

Step 2: Calculate the moles of the reactant (propyne).

We are given 4 g of propyne. We need its molar mass.

Propyne is C₃H₄. Molar Mass = 3 * (12.01 g/mol) + 4 * (1.01 g/mol) ≈ 36 + 4 = 40 g/mol.

Moles of propyne = Mass / Molar Mass = 4 g / 40 g/mol = 0.1 moles.

Step 3: Calculate the moles of the product (ammonia).

From the 1:1 stoichiometry, 0.1 moles of propyne will produce 0.1 moles of NH₃ gas.

Step 4: Calculate the volume of the gas at STP.

We have 0.1 moles of NH₃. We know that 1 mole at STP occupies 22400 mL.

Volume = Moles × Molar Volume = 0.1 mol × 22400 mL/mol = 2240 mL.

Step 5: Compare with the statement.

Our calculated volume is 2240 mL. The statement says the gas occupies 224 mL.

2240 mL ≠ 224 mL. The statement is off by a factor of 10.

Therefore, Statement II is incorrect.

Phase 3: The Summit - The JEE Main Question

Original Question: Given below are two statements :

Statement I : One mole of propyne reacts with excess of sodium to liberate half a mole of H₂ gas.

Statement II : Four g of propyne reacts with NaNH₂ to liberate NH₃ gas which occupies 224 mL at STP.

In the light of the above statements, choose the most appropriate answer from the options given below.

Let's summarize our findings from the previous phases.

Analysis of Statement I: We found that 2 moles of propyne produce 1 mole of H₂, so 1 mole of propyne produces 0.5 moles of H₂. Statement I is correct.

Analysis of Statement II: We found that 4g (0.1 mol) of propyne produces 0.1 mol of NH₃, which should occupy 2240 mL at STP, not 224 mL. Statement II is incorrect.

We are looking for the option that says Statement I is correct and Statement II is incorrect.

Final Answer Verification
