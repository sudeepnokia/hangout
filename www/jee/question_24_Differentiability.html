Phase 1: Differentiability of Piecewise Functions

For a piecewise function to be differentiable everywhere, it must be continuous everywhere, and the derivatives of the pieces must be equal at the point where they meet.

The function is: f(x) = -3ax²-2 for x<1 and f(x) = a²x+b for x≥1.

Question 1.1: What are the two conditions that must be met for f(x) to be differentiable at x=1?
Show Solution

1. Continuity: The values of the two pieces must be equal at x=1.

-3a(1)² - 2 = a²(1) + b => -3a - 2 = a² + b.

2. Equal Derivatives: The derivatives of the two pieces must be equal at x=1.

f'(x) for x<1 is -6ax. At x=1, this is -6a.

f'(x) for x≥1 is a². At x=1, this is a².

So, -6a = a².
