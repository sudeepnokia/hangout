Hyderabad Getaway - Option 3

A 3-Day Heritage, Nature & Quirky Finds Journey

Daily Itinerary

Old City Heritage Trail

Arrive in Hyderabad and check into your hotel.

Lunch at a famous biryani spot in RTC X Roads/Ashok Nagar

Visit the iconic Charminar

A historic monument and mosque, built in 1591, famous for its four minarets.

Visit the nearby Mecca Masjid

One of the oldest and largest mosques in India, built with soil from Mecca.

Explore Chowmahalla Palace

The magnificent palace of the Nizams, showcasing their opulent lifestyle and stunning architecture.

Dinner at Shadab for mutton delicacies

Approx. Daily Cost: ₹3,500

Forts, Tombs & Quirky Cars

Morning exploration of the majestic Golconda Fort

A historic fortress known for its acoustics and a former diamond trading center.

Visit the Qutb Shahi Tombs

Located near Golconda, these are the tombs of the rulers of the Qutb Shahi dynasty.

Lunch near Golconda/Tolichowki area.

Afternoon visit to Sudha Cars Museum

A quirky museum showcasing unique cars handmade by K. Sudhakar.

Night visit to Durgam Cheruvu Bridge

A modern cable-stayed bridge with beautiful lighting, offering stunning views of the lake.

Approx. Daily Cost: ₹4,000

Nature, Spirituality & Departure

Morning visit to Deer Park (Mahavir Harina Vanasthali)

A serene national park on the outskirts of the city, home to deer and other wildlife.

Lunch near the city center.

Relax at Lumbini Park by Hussain Sagar Lake

Enjoy the musical fountain and laser show in the evening.

Evening visit to the Birla Mandir

A stunning white marble temple overlooking the city, offering panoramic views.

Depart from Hyderabad.

Approx. Daily Cost: ₹2,500

Other Places to Explore

This itinerary is packed, but if you have extra time or want to swap an activity, here are other great options in Hyderabad:

Ramoji Film City

The world's largest film studio complex, offering tours, shows, and an amusement park. Requires a full day to explore.

Salar Jung Museum

Features one of the world's largest one-man collections of antiques, art, and artifacts from across the globe. Can be visited on Day 1 if time permits.

Hussain Sagar Lake

A large, heart-shaped lake with a famous Buddha statue in the middle. Great for an evening stroll, boat ride, and enjoying the city lights.
