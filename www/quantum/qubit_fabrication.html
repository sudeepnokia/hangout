Qubit Fabrication with Advanced VLSI Techniques

Concept Overview

This document explores the use of advanced Very Large Scale Integration (VLSI) techniques for the fabrication of qubits. By leveraging the precision and scalability of the semiconductor industry, we can aim to produce large arrays of high-quality, uniform qubits.

Pros

  • High Uniformity: VLSI processes enable precise and repeatable fabrication of qubits.
  • Massive Scalability: Can produce thousands or millions of qubits on a single wafer.
  • Cost Reduction: Leverages existing semiconductor infrastructure, potentially lowering costs.
  • Advanced Materials: Allows for the exploration and integration of novel materials for better qubit performance.

Cons

  • Material Compatibility: Not all qubit materials are compatible with standard CMOS fabrication processes.
  • Process-Induced Defects: Fabrication steps can introduce defects that harm qubit coherence.
  • Complexity of 3D Integration: Stacking layers of qubits and control electronics is extremely challenging.
  • Metrology and Testing: Characterizing and testing qubits at the wafer scale is a complex and slow process.
