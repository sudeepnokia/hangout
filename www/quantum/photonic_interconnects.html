Photonic Interconnects for Quantum Systems

Concept Overview

This document explores the use of Photonic Interconnects in the context of Very Large Scale Integration (VLSI) for quantum computing. The goal is to use light (photons) to transmit quantum information between different parts of a quantum computer, enabling modular and scalable architectures.

Pros

  • High Bandwidth: Optical links can carry vast amounts of information.
  • Low Crosstalk: Photons do not easily interfere with each other, reducing noise.
  • Room Temperature Operation: Interconnects can operate at room temperature, simplifying system design.
  • Modularity: Enables connecting multiple quantum processors to build a larger machine.

Cons

  • Transduction Inefficiency: Converting quantum information from electrical to photonic form (and back) is inefficient and noisy.
  • Photon Loss: Photons can be lost in the interconnect, leading to information loss.
  • Material Integration: Integrating photonic components with qubit materials is a major fabrication challenge.
  • Synchronization: Precisely timing the arrival of photons across different links is difficult.
