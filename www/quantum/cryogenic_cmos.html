Cryogenic CMOS for Quantum Computing

Concept Overview

This document explores the use of Cryogenic Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor (CMOS) technology in the context of Very Large Scale Integration (VLSI) for quantum computing. The core idea is to co-locate the classical control and readout electronics with the quantum bits (qubits) at cryogenic temperatures (typically below 4 Kelvin).

Pros

  • Reduced Latency: Placing control circuits close to qubits minimizes signal delay.
  • Improved Scalability: Leverages mature CMOS fabrication for complex controller designs.
  • Lower Noise: Shorter connections are less susceptible to noise interference.
  • Reduced Interconnect Complexity: Drastically cuts down the number of wires going from room temperature to the qubits.

Cons

  • Heat Dissipation: Even low-power CMOS can generate enough heat to disturb qubits.
  • Material Challenges: CMOS devices behave differently at cryogenic temperatures, requiring new models and characterization.
  • Limited Power Budget: The cooling power of cryostats is extremely limited at millikelvin temperatures.
  • Reliability: Thermal cycling can cause mechanical stress and reliability issues.
