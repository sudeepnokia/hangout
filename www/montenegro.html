Pre-Trip Preparation Checklist

3 Months Before (Late June) - Check Visa Requirements

As Indian passport holders, you generally need a visa for Montenegro. However, there's a key exception.
Action: Check with the nearest Montenegrin Embassy.
Important: You may enter Montenegro without a visa for up to 30 days if you hold a valid visa issued by the Schengen Area, USA, or United Kingdom.This can save you a significant amount of paperwork. Double-check this policy is still active before your travel dates. If not, you must apply for a national Montenegrin visa.

1 Week Before - Pack & Final Prep

Things to Pack:

  • Clothing: Layers are crucial.Coastal weather will be mild (15-22°C), but it can be much colder in the mountains, especially in the mornings and evenings. Pack t-shirts, a fleece, a waterproof jacket, and hiking trousers.
  • Footwear: Sturdy walking shoes or hiking boots are essential.
  • Essentials: Passport, visa/permit, insurance, universal adapter, power bank, Euro (€) cash.

Connectivity (SIM Card):

  • Local SIM: Buy at Tivat airport or in town. Providers like One, T-Mobile, or M:tel offer tourist packages (~€10-15 for ample data).
  • eSIM: Consider an eSIM for convenience.Activate it upon landing without a physical SIM. Check providers like Airalo or Holafly before you travel.
