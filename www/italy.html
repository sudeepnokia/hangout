Your Italian Odyssey

A 9-Day Grand Tour Through La Dolce Vita

Pre-Trip Preparation Checklist

2-3 Months Before (July) - Get Your Visa

As Indian passport holders, you are required to have a Schengen Visa to enter Italy.
Action: Apply for a Schengen visa through VFS Global.The process involves booking an appointment, submitting documents (flights, hotels, insurance), and biometrics. Start this process very early as appointments can be scarce.
Important: Your passport must be valid for at least 3 months beyond your intended departure from the Schengen area. Book refundable flights and hotels for your application.

2 Weeks Before - Pack & Final Prep

Things to Pack:

  • Clothing: Layers are key.Early autumn in Italy is pleasant (15-25°C), but can be cool in mornings/evenings. Pack t-shirts, light sweaters, a stylish jacket, and a scarf. A compact umbrella is wise. Modest attire (covering shoulders/knees) is mandatory for churches.
  • Footwear: Extremely comfortable walking shoes are non-negotiable.
  • Essentials: Passport, Visa, insurance, universal adapter (Type L), power bank, Euros (€) cash for small purchases.

Connectivity (SIM Card):

  • Local SIM: Buy at the airport (e.g., FCO) or a city store. TIM or Vodafone offer great coverage. A tourist pack costs ~€25-30.
  • eSIM: Consider an eSIM for instant connectivity.Activate it upon landing without a physical SIM. Check providers like Airalo or Holafly before you travel.

Your Flight Details

Onward: Sat, Sep 27

BLR → FCO (e.g., Emirates, Qatar Airways, etc.)

Fly into Rome's Fiumicino Airport (FCO). An overnight flight arriving in the morning is ideal.

Return: Sun, Oct 5

VCE → BLR (e.g., Emirates, Qatar Airways, etc.)

Fly out of Venice's Marco Polo Airport (VCE) to avoid backtracking. This is called an "open-jaw" ticket.
